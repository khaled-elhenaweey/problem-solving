<h2><a href=https://www.codewars.com/kata/563b662a59afc2b5120000c6/train/javascript target="_blank">Growth of a Population</a></h2><h3>7 kyu</h3><p data--h-bstatus="0OBSERVED">In a small town the population is <code data--h-bstatus="0OBSERVED">p0 = 1000</code> at the beginning of a year. The populationregularly increases by <code data--h-bstatus="0OBSERVED">2 percent</code> per year and moreover <code data--h-bstatus="0OBSERVED">50</code> new inhabitants per year come to live in the town. How many years does the town need to see its populationgreater than or equal to <code data--h-bstatus="0OBSERVED">p = 1200</code> inhabitants?</p><pre data--h-bstatus="0OBSERVED"><code data--h-bstatus="0OBSERVED">At the end of the first year there will be: 1000 + 1000 * 0.02 + 50 =&gt; 1070 inhabitantsAt the end of the 2nd year there will be: 1070 + 1070 * 0.02 + 50 =&gt; 1141 inhabitants (** number of inhabitants is an integer **)At the end of the 3rd year there will be:1141 + 1141 * 0.02 + 50 =&gt; 1213It will need 3 entire years.</code></pre><p data--h-bstatus="0OBSERVED">More generally given parameters:</p><p data--h-bstatus="0OBSERVED"><code data--h-bstatus="0OBSERVED">p0, percent, aug (inhabitants coming or leaving each year), p (population to equal or surpass)</code></p><p data--h-bstatus="0OBSERVED">the function <code data--h-bstatus="0OBSERVED">nb_year</code> should return <code data--h-bstatus="0OBSERVED">n</code> number of entire years needed to get a population greater or equal to <code data--h-bstatus="0OBSERVED">p</code>.</p><p data--h-bstatus="0OBSERVED">aug is an integer, percent a positive or null floating number, p0 and p are positive integers (&gt; 0)</p><pre data--h-bstatus="0OBSERVED"><code data--h-bstatus="0OBSERVED">Examples:nb_year(1500, 5, 100, 5000) -&gt; 15nb_year(1500000, 2.5, 10000, 2000000) -&gt; 10</code></pre><h4 id="note" data--h-bstatus="0OBSERVED">Note:</h4><ul data--h-bstatus="0OBSERVED"><li data--h-bstatus="0OBSERVED"><p data--h-bstatus="0OBSERVED">Don't forget to convert the percent parameter as a percentage in the body of your function: if the parameter percent is 2 you have to convert it to 0.02.</p></li><li data--h-bstatus="0OBSERVED"><p data--h-bstatus="0OBSERVED">There are no fractions of people. At the end of each year, the population count is an integer: <code data--h-bstatus="0OBSERVED">252.8</code> people round down to <code data--h-bstatus="0OBSERVED">252</code> persons.</p></li></ul>
